# Research

My research lies at the interface between statistical physics, disordered systems, and numerical simulations.

## Finite-temperature creep of elastic interfaces

I study the creep dynamics of driven elastic interfaces below the depinning threshold.

A central question in this problem is how thermally activated motion at very low velocity is organized in space and time. My recent work focuses on the separation between two distinct length scales: the thermal activation nucleus, which controls the Arrhenius timescale, and the avalanche scale, which controls spatial correlations after activation.

## Amorphous plasticity and elastoplastic models

I also work on coarse-grained elastoplastic models of amorphous plasticity, with a focus on localization, softening, shear banding, and thermally activated dynamics.

These models provide a mesoscopic description of deformation in disordered solids, combining local plastic rearrangements with long-range elastic interactions.

## Methods

My work combines numerical simulations, kinetic Monte Carlo methods, extremal dynamics, scaling analysis, and finite-size scaling.
